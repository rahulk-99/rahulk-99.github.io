About Me

Hi, I am Rahul Kumar. I am currently pursuing my Master's in Robotics Engineering at the University of Maryland, College Park (Exp. Graduation: May 2026). I am highly passionate about robotics, and My primary interests lie in Robot Learning (Reinforcement & Imitation Learning), Computer Vision, and Planning & Control for Autonomous Systems.

I earned my Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Dharwad, India. After that, I worked at Bharat Electronics Limited for 3.5 years as a Product Development Engineer in the R&D division, where I contributed to cutting-edge technological advancements.

I actively engage in brainstorming sessions and approach problems with a creative mindset and innovative ideas. Outside of academics and work, I enjoy playing table tennis and exploring music.

Feel free to check out my projects to learn more about my work and experiences.

LLM-Based Control for Autonomous Robot Navigation
  • Implemented a robot walking controller powered by LLMs using Few-Shot In-Context Learning, without requiring fine-tuning (inspired by paper).

  • Developing prompt caching and response reuse mechanism to reduce per-step inference latency in real-time control loops.
Path Planning: A-Star vs Dijkstra Algorithm
  • Implements Dijkstra's and A* algorithms for robot pathfinding in a 2D environment, visualizing node exploration (green), clearance zones (yellow), obstacles (red), and the final path (blue).
  • A* reduces the number of nodes explored by approximately 75% compared to Dijkstra, using a heuristic-guided search to prioritize nodes closer to the goal.

  • Extended the DQN framework for 3D robotic arm control in Gazebo using ROS2, enabling real-time deployment with a Baxter robot.
